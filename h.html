Welcome to I-EDI's new English site: 2023 updates in progress
I-EDI is the English branch of 傈僳联合社区开发会, with growing global activities in English as we restructure to meet needs during and after the COVID-19 pandemic. With our roots in international development, I-EDI aims to support equality, through supporting:

1. People living with disabilities
2. Survivors/victims of trauma
3. Marginalized communities - indigenous, or rural
4. Individuals experiencing multiple disadvantages listed above

I-EDI digital contracts support the employment or resume-building of individuals living with disabilities, trauma, or marginalized-status. Although these contracts are at a much lower employment-level than our aims for individuals, these contracts are: 
- fully paid
- tenable anywhere around the world with internet access
- open to individuals with any level of formal education
- designed to recognizing existing talents of individuals, with or without previous formal employment experience

Some contracts pay for past (completed) work. 

To simplify the application process, individuals can submit their portfolio of work and automatically matched for any new opportunities. 

To Submit Your Portfolio to Digital Contracts, please submit resume and links to work by email to:

I-EDI
admin@i-edi.org

We specifically invite applicants who experience barriers in traditional, face-to-face or on-site employment.

You may wish to self-disclose as much or as little information about your situation, as IEDI prioritizes paid contracts as a supportive tool to accomplish equality in access-to-income. Please note all disclosed information will be verified during interview.
