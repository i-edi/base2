Grant Administration 2022~2023

Since 2015, the grant provides direct-purchase assistance especially in the lowest-income and highest-need sub-communities.
Originally covering the Lisu region across China, northern Thailand, northern India, Myanmar, and Tibet, the grant is now expanded for international collaborations.

Competition Details & Eligibility

We specifically invite researchers, practitioners, or students who themselves face health and/or social disadvantages as outlined on our home page. If you are a researcher, practitioner, or student with a research or community-based, participatory project that seeks to assist other individuals facing health and/or social disadvantages, we strongly encourage you to apply.

Details:

Fiscal Deadline: March 1st of every year
Application Status: Open
Amount: Assessed need, but up to a maximum of 10000 USD in direct purchasing support per project per year
Criteria: we specifically invite applications seeking to serve hard-to-access populations; long-standing research gaps and unmet needs; projects ineligible for other funding due to unique health and/or social disadvantage, or socio-political contexts

Applicants who have previously applied for this grant, or have Mandarin-language proficiency may download and complete the existing grant application form.

First-time applicants are encouraged to submit an initial letter-of-inquiry, which may be directly in the email body. In the letter-of-inquiry, provide as many details as you can about the project without any attachments or addendums, and consider the following questions:

1. What direct purchases am I asking for, and how do these direct purchases improve equal access to resources for high-need sub-populations?
2. Am I willing to allow the grant committee to perform the direct purchase on my behalf?
3. What is the approximate number of individuals who will benefit from these direct purchases, and what is my methodology of estimation?
4. What is the approximate number of individuals who will indirectly benefit, or benefit in the future?
5. Which organizations or partners could I contact to submit a collective application?
6. Why are the direct purchases I am proposing the most strategic, beneficial, or fundamental?
7. What is the short-term or long-term outcome I wish to see?
8. Which suppliers or vendors will I purchase from, and have I considered multiple sources to compare prices?
9. How will I deliver or ship the resources to the individuals who will benefit from my project?
10. Most importantly, among the sub-population my project hopes to serve, are there further hidden/marginalized/multi-disadvantaged individuals who are excluded, intentionally or unintentionally, due to the design or limitations of the project? How will I seek to address the inequalities within the sub-population?

There is no limit on length for the initial letter-of-inquiry.

If you do not receive notice that we have received your application or email within 7 days, kindly inquire again.
If you require accessibility-support to complete the application, our accessibility committee will be happy to be in touch.
