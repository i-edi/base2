Institute
for Equality
in Development Innovations

With its roots in rural indigenous community development, the Institute of Equality in Development Innovations provides bi-lingual funding, knowledge mobilization, and research training.

The IEDI's research seeks to reduce experiences of inequality for:

1. People living with disabilities (and their family/caregivers)
2. People living with chronic health conditions, including mental health challenges (and their family/caregivers)
3. People representing many indigenous nationalities around the world
4. People who are displaced, or limited to rural and/or marginalized areas with unequal access to education
5. Special individuals who experience multiple challenges listed above.

Our mission is to support individuals who experience inequality and/or disadvantages when pursuing research or teaching positions. We believe lived experience with a social or health disadvantage matters, and can serve as an invaluable teaching and research background to educate new generations.

We also believe in equal access to teaching and research positions, specifically in fields related to health and social disadvantage (such as OT/PT; medicine research; social sciences) and these positions, when offered to individuals with direct lived experience of disadvantage, help communities begin to break cycles of poverty.

Since 2015, we jointly administer a research-trainee grant, which is open to all researchers, practitioners, or student-leaders whose proposed research assists other disadvantaged members of communities around the world.

Our new English-based journal, Equality: The Review, emphasizes voices and lived experience of health and/or social disadvantage. We specifically want to extend the invitation to community organizations, student-leaders, and all practitioners with or without formal recognition of experience.

The IEDI collaborates with partners to offer paid, accessible contracts to trainees with professional level of expertise.

IEDI's masters and PhD programs seeks to fill the gap of accessible, tuition-covered higher education opportunities - for students and family members who may experience unequal access to higher education.
