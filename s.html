I-EDI SafeSpace is an open-access, streamlined experience - inviting individuals to share their insights into issues relating to equality or injustices.

I-EDI SafeSpace seeks to bridge 2 existing gaps:

1) Interdisciplinary: At the regional and local levels, individuals may be able to access different cost-free services such as helplines, whether targeted at legal advice, social welfare, disability rehabilitation, or mental health, etc. Yet oftentimes, these services may be strictly defined in scope as part of funding constraints, leaving out discussions which extend beyond traditionally-defined academic boundaries
2) Equal-power: I-EDI acknowledges that in some cases, individuals are not looking for "help", but rather, a place to share thoughts, experience, and personal insights, where they are heard in positions of equal-power in a discussion. While help-seeking is accommodated or socially-accepted, there is no clear answer as to where an individual can seek out engaging discussions where they are not looking to be helped, but simply respected and listened to.

I-EDI SafeSpace invites all individuals to connect via the following link: I-EDI SafeSpace First Contact

All individuals are welcome - this is a place where personal experiences can be shared. Beyond personal experiences, if you are moved and compelled by inequalities or injustices you see affecting people around you, your community, or simply other human beings, I-EDI SafeSpace welcomes you.

Conflicting or negative emotions are welcome, though we also extend invites to positive, collaborative, or even action-oriented discussions.
