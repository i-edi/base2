Collaborate towards Action

The IEDI's research seeks to reduce inequality that often times are invisible

Historically, our research emphasizes improving direct access for communities as the main research output. A research project should improve equitable access for community members - in the form of access to health care, access to education, access to employment, access to life opportunities, or access to community and mental wellbeing. If after a research project team leaves a community - and access is not improved, we do not prioritize this situation no matter how many academic research outputs (papers, conference presentations) can be generated.

From a theoretical perspective, IEDI's research leans towards "action-oriented", "project-based", or "participatory-action research". Others may consider this to not be research at all, but rather NGO or charity work. IEDI believes the latter labels are positive.

If you have an interest in research that crosses the boundary from academic outputs for the researcher, to tangible, beneficial inputs for the lives of the participants - please be in touch. We look forward to collaborating with you!
